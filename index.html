About Me

Hey there! I’m Jeofroi Correya, a full stack web developer who writes code that actually works (most days). I specialize in building clean, responsive front-ends and reliable back-ends — basically the kind of apps that don’t fall apart when someone clicks too fast. I didn’t take the usual college-to-cubicle route — dropped the textbook life, chose the terminal, and dove into full stack development like it owed me money. Currently mastering the art of writing functional code while silently battling semicolons and unnecessary bugs (a.k.a. features that weren’t invited). I work with HTML, CSS, JavaScript, C++, Java, and Python — or as I like to call it, the survival kit for modern developers. I’ve handled real clients, real deadlines, and real bugs that somehow only appear right before demos. It builds character. And anxiety. Beyond code, I’ve got experience in customer support, so I’m basically trained in debugging both software and human emotions. Not to brag, but I’ve calmed down more angry users than most fire extinguishers. When I’m off the clock, you’ll find me geeking out over superheroes, planning food business ideas I’ll launch after 17 side projects, or pretending to fix “just one last bug” at 3 a.m. (Spoiler: it was never just one.) I believe in clean code, clear logic, dark mode, and not shipping anything that I wouldn't be proud to show my future self (or my dog, if I had one).
